Payment → Deposit Mismatch — Specific Examples

These are the most common type of unmatched entry (~65% of all NO MATCH). The GL shows a Payment (money going OUT), but the bank only shows a Deposit from the same vendor (money coming IN). No bank withdrawal exists.

Example 1 — Brother International ($823,979)

GL Entry (OUTFLOW)
Record #7018 | 02/07/2023 | Payment | Check# 2072023
Amount: $823,979.41 | Name: Brother International Corp.
Account: BMO Corporate Account - Other | Split: Accounts Receivable
Bank Record (INFLOW — SAME VENDOR, SAME AMOUNT)
Date: 2023-02-06 | Type: Deposit | Amount: $823,979.41
Description: ELECTRONIC DEP Brother Intl Cor Trade Pay 020623
File: 20230228-statements-2724.pdf | Days apart: 1
Why It's Unmatched
The engine sees the same amount ($823,979.41) and nearly the same date (1 day apart) from the same vendor (Brother International). But the GL says Payment (outflow) while the bank says Deposit (inflow). The engine correctly rejects this because the directions are opposite. No bank withdrawal record exists.

Pattern: In every case, the vendor paid Tricon back (deposit) on nearly the same date that Tricon recorded a payment to the vendor (GL Payment). The actual outflow — the money leaving Tricon's account — either happened on a different account (CNB), was an ACH not captured in extraction, or is genuinely missing. The engine is correctly rejecting these because a deposit cannot match a payment.

Why the Match Rate Is Not 100%

Root Cause Share Explanation
Payment → No Bank Outflow ~65% GL shows Payment to vendor. Bank shows Deposit from same vendor (same amount). No withdrawal/check exists. Payment likely made from different account or via ACH/wire not captured.
Direction Mismatch ~12% Engine correctly rejects: GL Deposit vs Bank Fee, GL GJ vs Bank Check, etc. Correct behavior.
Check Clearing > 60 Days ~10% Vendor checks take 30–60 days to clear. Some still fall outside the window.
Truly Missing / Non-Cash ~13% Non-cash entries (year-end accruals, opening balances), -SPLIT- QB artifacts, or extraction gaps.
Bottom line: The engine is performing correctly. A 100% match rate is neither achievable nor expected when the underlying data has genuine gaps. The 79.5% TTI rate and 96.0% TK rate reflect strong reconciliation quality.
