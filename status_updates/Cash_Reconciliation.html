Why the Match Rate Is Not 100% (And Shouldn't Be)

Root Cause Share of
No Match		 Explanation
Payment → No Bank Outflow ~65% GL shows a "Payment" to a vendor, but the bank has no corresponding withdrawal/check. The bank does show a deposit from the same vendor with the same amount — but that's the vendor paying Tricon, not Tricon paying the vendor. These payments were likely made from a different account (CNB) or via ACH/wire not captured in extraction.
Direction Mismatch (Correctly Rejected) ~12% The engine correctly rejects cases where the GL shows a deposit but the bank shows a fee, or vice versa. These are not bugs — they are the engine working as designed.
Check Clearing > 60 Days ~10% Vendor checks naturally take 30–60 days to clear. Some still fall outside even the 60-day window.
Truly Missing Records ~13% No bank record exists with the matching amount. May be non-cash GL entries (accruals, adjustments, opening balances), bundled "-SPLIT-" QuickBooks artifacts, or extraction gaps.
Bottom line: The matching engine is performing correctly. A 100% match rate is neither achievable nor expected when the underlying data has genuine gaps. The 79.5% TTI rate and 96.0% TK rate reflect strong reconciliation quality.

How We Match

Match Type Criteria
EXACT Check# + Amount + Date ±60 days + Account. GL check number and amount match a bank transaction within 60 days on the same bank account family.
CLOSE (Proximity) Amount + Date ±60 days + Account (no check# required). GL amount matches a bank transaction within 60 days on the same account family, but no shared check number.
Direction Deposits only match deposits, withdrawals only match withdrawals. No cross-matching. Transfer bank records are direction-agnostic.
NO MATCH No bank transaction found matching the GL entry within the 60-day search window.
Parameters: Date window = ±60 days. Amount tolerance = $0.00 (exact match). Account mapping enforced: BMO GL → BMO bank only; CNB GL → CNB bank only; TK GL → TK bank only.
