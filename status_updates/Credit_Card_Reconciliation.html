Executive Summary

TTI uses two American Express cards — Business Platinum and Personal Platinum — with combined annual spend of ~$575K. The company accounts for these on a cash basis: the GL payment TO American Express IS the expense entry. There is no separate expense accrual or active credit card liability account.

When TTI writes a check to AMEX from BMO or CNB, QuickBooks debits expense accounts and credits cash via a single -SPLIT- transaction. This pattern accounts for 85.6% of AMEX GL entries.

Total AMEX spend (statements): $1.28M across 2024–Q1 2026 | Total AMEX payments (GL): $2.53M across 2023–2026 (includes prior-year balances). 2023 AMEX statements were not provided — the $817K in 2023 GL payments cannot be verified against source documents.

Reconciliation Boundaries: What Can & Cannot Be Tied

Because TTI uses cash basis accounting, the GL never registers individual transactions — only bulk payments. Normal month-to-month timing variances of $0–$10K are expected.

Can TieCannot Tie
Monthly AMEX statement totals → check in BMO/CNB bank data Individual AMEX transactions to specific GL entries (only bulk payment shows up)
Total annual statement spend → total annual GL payments (~1 month timing gap) Bookkeeper's -SPLIT- category allocation to actual merchant spend (QB estimation)
Large one-off charges (e.g., Rusnak Porsche) to cash account movement

Accounting Method: Cash Basis via -SPLIT-

TTI does not record individual AMEX transactions as they occur. The monthly cycle:

— Employee swipes AMEX (Day 1-30)

— AMEX issues monthly statement

— TTI writes single check for statement balance

— Check clears BMO or CNB

— QB auto-allocates total via -SPLIT- across expense accounts

2. AMEX Liability Account & TAX AJE #8

The "American Express" balance sheet account was an orphan from prior accounting periods (holding a -$72,313 balance in 2023). On 12/31/2024, TAX AJE #8 zeroed out this account alongside a MasterCard liability account ($27,114) and a Freightliner loan account ($67,145), offsetting them against Advanced Payments and a Line of Credit.

This was a reclassification, not a cash payment. Supporting rationale is needed from the client's tax team.

3. MasterCard Inactivity

MasterCard is effectively inactive in the GL. Only three entries exist across all years, culminating in the TAX AJE #8 reclassification. No statements are required.

4. General QoE Observations

— Cash basis accounting means statement-to-GL tie is inherently approximate — the GL never sees individual transactions, only bulk payments. Month-to-month variance of $0–$10K is normal timing noise.

— No receipt/documentation trail was pulled from the statements. For QoE purposes, the AMEX statement ITSELF is the supporting document.

— "Other" category at $226K (17.6%) is large but composed of small individual charges. No single merchant exceeds $5K outside the named categories.

— 2023 statements missing. If they become available, category table should be extended backward and the $817K in 2023 payments verified.
