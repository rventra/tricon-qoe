Executive Summary

TTI uses two American Express cards — Business Platinum and Personal Platinum — with combined annual spend of ~$575K. The company accounts for these on a cash basis: the GL payment TO American Express IS the expense entry. There is no separate expense accrual. When TTI writes a check to AMEX from BMO or CNB, QuickBooks debits expense accounts and credits cash — all in one transaction via the -SPLIT- mechanism. 85.6% of AMEX GL entries use this pattern.

Total AMEX spend (from statements): $1.28M across 2024–Q1 2026 | Total AMEX payments in GL: $2.53M across 2023–2026 (includes prior-year balances)

Accounting Method: Cash Basis via -SPLIT-

TTI does not record individual AMEX transactions as they occur. The monthly cycle:

— Employee swipes AMEX (Day 1-30)

— AMEX issues monthly statement

— TTI writes single check for statement balance

— Check clears BMO or CNB

— QB auto-allocates total via -SPLIT- across expense accounts

QoE Observations

— Cash basis accounting means statement-to-GL tie is inherently approximate — the GL never sees individual transactions, only bulk payments. Month-to-month variance of $0–$10K is normal timing noise.

— Personal Platinum has ~$30K in clearly personal/non-operating spend across 2024–2026 (firearms, collectibles, concerts, Vegas hotels, airfare). This is on a company card — normalization/adjustment discussion needed with client.

— No receipt/documentation trail was extracted from the statements. For QoE purposes, the AMEX statement ITSELF is the supporting document.

— "Other" category at $226K (17.6%) is large but composed of small individual charges. No single merchant exceeds $5K outside the named categories.

— TAX AJE #8 reclassified $72K AMEX liability without cash movement. Need tax team rationale for the reclassification.
