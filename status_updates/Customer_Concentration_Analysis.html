§2 — QoE Discoveries from the Aggregate Data

2.1 The McDonald's Ecosystem is the True Anchor

  • The CIM states Tricon's largest client is a supply chain solutions provider serving a well-known fast-food brand with ~13,000 domestic locations. That is Armada.
  • The CIM also notes a long-standing working affiliation with a closely related entity, "Client 5." That is HAVI. Together, Armada and HAVI manage the global McDonald's supply chain.
  • The Risk: Combined, the Armada + HAVI ecosystem represents roughly $23.1M in aggregate historical revenue (~41.5% of total). If McDonald's shifts its supply chain logistics strategy, Tricon's top-line revenue would be severely impacted.
  • The Mitigant: Armada is aggressively utilizing the TTIM and TTIO warehouses, validating the CIM's claim of a multi-year, cost-plus facility contract. The warehousing relationship provides stickiness beyond pure transportation.

2.2 Becton Dickinson (BD) is the Drayage Engine

  • The CIM notes Client 2 is a global medical technology firm that was among the company's first clients. The data confirms Becton Dickinson is an absolute machine for the transportation side of the business.
  • Unlike Armada (which leans heavily on warehousing), BD spends millions exclusively on Container Drayage and Chassis Rent. They are the primary reason Tricon keeps its fleet of 17 vehicles and 130 chassis moving.
  • AR Risk Flag: BD has $78K in stale >90-day receivables across multiple years — the largest single bad-debt exposure in the AR waterfall. Monitor collection closely.

2.3 TOTO/VYMI & Honda: The Diversification Play

  • The data validates the CIM's claims regarding a diversified client base of consumer goods and prominent automobile manufacturers.
  • TOTO (global plumbing/hardware manufacturer) and American Honda are heavily utilizing the Inland Empire facility (TTIO), proving that expansion into San Bernardino County was a successful strategic move to capture non-food-grade industrial clients.
  • These clients represent lower concentration risk than the McDonald's ecosystem and provide a natural hedge against food-service sector volatility.

§3 — What We Still Need: Gross Margin by Customer

Revenue concentration tells us who writes the biggest checks, but margin concentration tells us who actually pays the bills. Sometimes the #1 client by revenue is so big they demand massive volume discounts, meaning they might represent 35% of revenue but only 15% of gross profit. Conversely, a smaller client like TOTO might be highly profitable.

3.1 Why We Need It

  • Armada's margin profile is unknown. If Armada's warehousing is cost-plus (as the CIM suggests), the margin may be thin but stable. If drayage rates are heavily discounted, the profit contribution could be lower than the revenue share implies.
  • BD's drayage margin is critical. Container drayage is a high-volume, low-margin business. We need to know whether BD's $8.1M in revenue is a margin hero or a margin drag.
  • Warehouse vs. Drayage profitability. The two business lines have fundamentally different cost structures. We need to separate them by customer to assess true economic contribution.

3.2 The Data Gap: Load-Level Detail

  • What we need: A detailed line-by-line load manifest or order-level report from 3PL Central by Extensiv for 2023–2025.
  • Why it works: Every time a container is moved, the system generates a Load ID. That Load ID ties the Client (e.g., Becton Dickinson) directly to the Carrier (e.g., subcontractor) and the specific Chassis / Port Fees incurred for that exact trip.
  • The output: Pivot the raw data to build a perfect COGS-by-Customer profile. Match revenue per customer (we already have this) against direct costs per customer (we need this) to get true gross margin.

3.3 Request for Client

Action item: Request the following from Tricon's operations team:

  1. 3PL Central (Extensiv) export: all orders / loads for 2023, 2024, and 2025
  2. Required fields per row: Load ID, Date, Customer Name, Service Type (Drayage / Warehouse / Storage), Revenue Amount, Carrier/Subcontractor Cost, Chassis Fee, Port Fee
  3. If 3PL Central cannot export this, request the equivalent WMS report or dispatch log
