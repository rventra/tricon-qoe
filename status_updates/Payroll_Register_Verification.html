Payroll Register Verification

BBSi PEO Tie-Out  |  TTI, TK, TWS  |  2023–2025  |  Prepared: May 2026

Overview

The payroll register tie-out tests whether stated payroll expenses are accurate, actually paid, and free of hidden costs. We compare BBSi Payment Check Allocation Reports (PDF registers) against the General Ledger and bank statements in a three-way tie: Register → GL Expense → GL Cash.

The GL tie is solid. GL Expense matches GL Cash across every year and entity — Leg 2 is clean. The variances are between the PDF register and the GL, driven by explainable items (pre-BBSi accruals, WC premiums, reimbursements) rather than missing or phantom payroll.

Key findings: TTI 2024–2025 ties perfectly. TTI 2023 has a $42K variance from pre-BBSi accruals. TK 2024–2025 ties perfectly; TK 2023 includes $762K in WC premiums bundled with payroll. TWS register data is available but GL mapping is missing. Q1 2026 registers have not yet been received.

2024 and 2025 tie perfectly. 2023 has a $42K variance from pre-BBSi accruals ($15,640 non-cash AP entries + $26,321 year-end timing). The P&L for 2023 shows a -$351K gap vs the register — likely because the P&L only captured the BBSi period (Sep–Dec), missing pre-BBSi payroll booked through different accounts.
