QoE Observations

1. Revenue is highly collectible. Implied cash ($55,852,709) matches actual cash collections ($55,856,625) within $3,916 (99.993%). This is strong evidence that TTI's revenue is real, from credit-worthy customers, and properly recorded.

2. $756K unclassified revenue needs review. 1.4% of total revenue activity could not be matched to invoices or standard deposit patterns. Possible explanations: (a) intercompany revenue between TTI and TK, (b) misc income (fuel surcharges, detention fees), (c) data entry errors where invoice numbers were omitted.

3. $132K "Does not Belong" requires reclassification. These are non-revenue items sitting in revenue accounts. For QoE purposes, they should be backed out of revenue and reclassified to the appropriate account (intercompany, expense reimbursement, or contra-revenue).

4. No deferred revenue = simple revenue recognition. TTI does not carry deferred revenue, meaning all revenue is recognized at the point of billing (or deposit). This simplifies the QoE analysis — there are no long-term contracts or prepayments to unwind.
