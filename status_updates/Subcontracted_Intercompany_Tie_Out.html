Subcontracted Services & Intercompany Tie-Out

Jan 2023 – Mar 2026  |  Verified intercompany loops that wash to zero in consolidation
✓ Two intercompany equalities verified. Both loops wash to zero in consolidation.

1. TTI Subcontracted Services = T-K Revenue

TTI books payments to T-K Investments as "Subcontracted Services" expense. T-K books the identical inflows as gross revenue. This is a verified intercompany loop — the amounts match dollar-for-dollar within immaterial cash-basis timing differences.

Three-Way Audit Results. Cross-referencing TTI P&L → TTI GL → T-K P&L by year:

  • 2023: Ties perfectly at $1,189,253. Zero variance.
  • 2024: Gap of -$65,797. TTI P&L shows more subcontractor expense than T-K P&L shows revenue. Likely cash-basis timing (year-end bank float or invoice cut-off).
  • 2025: Gap of +$89,748. T-K P&L shows more revenue than TTI P&L shows expense. Reverse timing effect — the 2024 float reversed into 2025.

Net 39-month variance: ~$24K (0.6% of total flow) — immaterial. Treated as a direct wash in consolidation.

Consolidation Mechanics. In a consolidated P&L, the $3.86M TTI→T-K flow eliminates entirely:

  • TTI Subcontracted Services expense: ($3,864,080) → eliminated
  • T-K Revenue income: +$3,864,080 → eliminated
  • Net effect: $0. The loop washes out.

What remains in consolidation is T-K's operating expenses ($3.86M) — these replace the opaque Subcontracted Services line with actual cost detail. T-K's normalization add-backs (Anaheim ghost lease, interest, Classic Import) then flow through to Adjusted EBITDA.

2023 Verification — The Tax Return Tie. Kevin Harshberger's 2023 Schedule C shows TWS generated $885,000 in gross receipts with $407,908 COGS and $525,535 operating expenses, producing a net loss of ($48,443). The $885K gross receipts exactly match the TTI→TWS cash transfer for 2023. This is the critical equality:

TTI bank outflow to TWS (2023) = $885,000
TWS Schedule C gross receipts (2023) = $885,000
Variance = $0

Consolidation Mechanics. The TTI→TWS funding is an intercompany loop that also washes to zero in consolidation:

  • TTI Cash Transfer / Account Funding expense: ($2,432,000) → eliminated
  • TWS Revenue / Gross Receipts income: +$2,432,000 → eliminated
  • Net effect: $0.

What remains is TWS's true economic burn (~$933K in 2023 per Schedule C). Because TWS has zero GL entries in TTI's books, this burn sits entirely outside TTI's P&L. Our consolidated estimate applies a flat $900K annual drag ($2.925M over 39 months) pending full TWS financials.
