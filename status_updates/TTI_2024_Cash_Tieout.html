TTI Cash Tie-Out

2023 through 2026  |  GL to Bank  |  Prepared: May 2026

The Big Picture

Over four years, TTI recorded $127M in cash-related GL entries. Of that, $97.6M (about 77%) matched cleanly to bank records.

2023 and 2024 are in solid shape — both years hit 90% or better, with 2024 coming in strongest at 94%. 2025 drops to 29%, but that's not a red flag in the books. It's a data problem: the CNB statements came over as image-only compilation PDFs, and roughly 950 check details never made it into our extraction. The BMO account itself still matched at 88%.

2026 is basically untestable right now — no bank statements have arrived yet. That's a timeline issue, not an audit concern.

What's Driving the Gaps?

2023 — $3.2M unmatched (281 items)

Two main things:

— Outstanding checks ($1.5M): Carrier checks that hadn't cleared by the time we ran the match. Some cleared outside our 45-day window, others were written from BMO-TTIPark which doesn't map to a TTI GL cash account.

— Deposit timing ($1.1M): A mix of QuickBooks bundled deposits and month-end timing differences.

2024 — $2.6M unmatched (173 items)

The big one here is a $1.6M year-end tax journal entry — it's non-cash, so there's no bank transaction to match. Take that out and the true gap drops to about $1.0M.

Of the remainder, $800K is outstanding checks (carrier and payroll checks still in flight), plus a handful of ACH settlements and bill payments.

2025 — $17.5M unmatched (2,181 items)

This looks scary, but it's almost entirely a data extraction issue on the CNB side.

The 2025 CNB statements arrived as a 12-page image-only compilation. The detailed "Checks Paid" continuation pages — the ones that actually list check numbers, dates, and amounts — were not extracted. Roughly 950 checks cleared the bank but never made it into our dataset.

The BMO account, meanwhile, matched at a healthy 88.4%. Once we get proper CNB detail, the overall rate should climb back into the 90s.

2026 — $10.6M unmatched (1,073 items)

Simple: no bank statements have been received yet. The 7 "matches" we did find are coincidental — same amounts, same dates, but not verified. Everything else is just waiting on paperwork.

How We Match

We run two passes:

— Exact: Same check number + same amount + within about 45 days.

— Close: Same amount + close date, even if the check number doesn't line up.

Everything else gets flagged as a gap. We search across all four years at once, so a December 2023 check that clears in January 2024 still has a shot at matching.

Bottom Line

— 2023 and 2024 are clean. Match rates in the 90s, gaps driven by normal timing and one big non-cash journal entry.

— 2025 is a data problem, not a books problem. ~950 missing CNB checks are artificially dragging the rate down. BMO side is fine.

— 2026 is waiting on statements. Nothing to worry about yet — just need the paperwork.
