Executive Summary

The vendor and freight audit is complete. AP Aging is highly clean with an immaterial ~$115K total balance, confirming that vendor payments are processed almost entirely on a cash basis with no complex invoice pass-through structures or unrecorded liabilities.

The primary QoE adjustments focus on (1) eliminating massive internal cash transfers misclassified as operational expenses, (2) reclassifying outsourced freight to match industry-standard Gross Margin presentation, and (3) calculating the precise unrealized incremental runway for the warehouse optimization strategy to avoid double-counting savings already captured on the ledger.

1. Cash-Basis & Intercompany Realities

Historically, the company's standalone ledger blended direct operational costs, intercompany movements, and facility real estate into single-line accounts.

$1.5M TWS Intercompany Cash Loop. The bookkeeper systematically misclassified $1,505,000 of pure cash transfers (moving funds from the TTI master account to the TWS Chase account) as operational expenses within the TTIM and TTIO real estate accounts. To present true historical cash flow, these are treated as a definitive EBITDA add-back:

  • $885,000 in 2023
  • $500,000 in 2024
  • $120,000 in 2025

$3.86M T-K Investments Loop. Within TTI’s standalone COGS, $3.86M was categorized as Subcontracted Services. Our deep-dive verified this is entirely a cash transfer loop to the owners’ separate pass-through entity (T-K Investments). These are not third-party vendor expenses and must be completely eliminated prior to consolidating T-K’s actual driver payroll.

Cash-Basis Real Estate Distortion. True third-party facility costs reside strictly in the TTIM and TTIO accounts. Because these are maintained on a strict cash basis, EBITDA was artificially distorted by timing errors:

  • +$100,000 (2023): Capitalized security deposit paid to REG Logistics (TTIO) back to the Balance Sheet from the P&L.
  • +$120,000 (2025): Capitalized security deposit paid to Prologis (TTIM) back to the Balance Sheet from the P&L.
  • −$120,000 (2024): Accrued rent expense for Sept/Oct 2024 to account for periods where the Pacific Expressway security deposit was applied to base rent in lieu of cash payments.
  • −$61,889 (Q1 2026): Accrued base rent for January 2026 at TTIM to ensure accurate expense cutoff for the period.

824 Anaheim — Ghost Lease (T-K Investments). This rent expense does not map to any known facility on the official Tricon Property Leases schedule (Corporate HQ, Food Grade Warehouse, General Warehouse West, or General Warehouse East). It is a personal/out-of-footprint expense run through the T-K Investments driver payroll entity. Because T-K is a break-even pass-through, this cost is invisible in TTI standalone but surfaces in consolidation. Confirmed add-back: $64,090/year ($16,023/quarter). See the EBITDA Normalization page for the full T-K consolidation bridge.
